Research Interests

    My research interest lies in the broad fields of statistics, applied probability, machine learning and optimization.
    I mainly work on both the theory and applications of experimental design and causal inference. On the theoretical side, I provide statistical analysis and inference for randomized experiments, utilizing tools such as asymptotic statistics, stochastic optimization and multiple testing; As for practical applications, I combine machine learning algorithms with online experiments to improve the accuracy and efficiency of large-scale A/B tests within prominent tech enterprises such as Amazon and Tencent. I am also interested in the interferences and fairness problems in experimental design.
    Before coming to Berkeley, I used to work for a long time on stochastic filtering and large deviation expansions, with their applications to financial econometrics, but my research interest has substantially changed.
