I am a first-year Ph.D. student in IEOR at the University of California, Berkeley, advised by Professor Zeyu Zheng. Previously, I received my B.Sc. in Statistics in 2021 from School of Mathematical Science, Peking University, where I was also a member of Elite Program for Applied Mathematics and Statistics. I finished my undergraduate thesis on asymptotics of stochastic filtering under Professor Chenxu Li's advice. I have also worked in Prof. Zaiwen Wen's group on stochastic optimization.

Research Interests

    My research interest lies in the broad field of statistics, applied probability and machine learning.
    My current focus is on statistical analysis for online experiments, bandits, and reinforcement learning. Besides, I'm also interested in causal inference.
